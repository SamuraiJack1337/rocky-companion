How Rocky sees your world

So Rocky can react to what you are up to, Rocky takes a quiet look at your screen from time to time — about every 15 minutes by default (you can change this, or pause it, anytime).

  • By default Rocky reacts to what he actually sees, so his remarks can mention what's on screen. Prefer he only knows your activity at a high level (coding, writing, taking a break)? Switch to Classic remarks in Settings and he never reads specific text.
  • Either way, screens that look private (logins, banking, messages, personal documents) never get a specific remark.
  • No images are ever saved. Each screenshot lives in memory for a moment, then is gone.
  • You can pause Rocky or quit at any time from the menu bar.
Rocky's pledge: screenshots are never written to disk, and your API key is never logged.

Who looks at the screen

Choose where the high-level analysis happens. Local keeps everything on your device.

Local setup (Ollama)

Rocky will talk to Ollama running on your machine.

Local mode accepts loopback hosts only.

Must be vision-capable. Lighter picks (moondream, gemma3:4b) load faster.

Cloud setup (OpenAI)

With cloud, each screenshot is sent to OpenAI under your own API key for analysis. The on-device option keeps screenshots local; cloud does not. Your key is validated, then stored encrypted in your Mac's keychain — it is never written to a file or logged.
